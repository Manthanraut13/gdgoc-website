10

UX Principles Used 8 core principles visible across the design decisions

🎯

Social Proof First

10,000+ participants, 35+ speakers, LinkedIn testimonials, and photo galleries all front-loaded to establish credibility instantly.

📣

Urgency Without Pressure

"WOW 2026 — Apply Now" messaging creates forward momentum. The 3-year journey narrative builds FOMO without aggressive countdowns.

🔠

Progressive Disclosure

FAQ Terminal reveals answers one at a time. Year timeline requires selection. LinkedIn posts truncated with "...more." All reduce cognitive load.

🎨

Visual Identity Borrowing

Google's 4-color palette signals legitimacy and authority. Attendees subconsciously associate WOW Pune with Google's brand quality.

🧑‍💻

Audience-Native Design

Terminal-style FAQ, monospace typography, and technical "v2.0" labels speak the language of the developer audience natively.

📊

Numbers as Trust Signals

Stat numbers (10K+, 35+, 25+) used 3× — in year card, impact section, and hero stats. Repetition anchors perceived scale.

🔄

Dual CTA Strategy

Two distinct conversion paths: "Apply as Speaker" (contribute) and "Become a Sponsor" (invest). Both addressable on same page without conflict.

🌓

Contrast as Navigation

Dark footer, light body, tinted sponsor section, green newsletter band — section color alternation creates visual chapter breaks without explicit dividers.

Strongest UX Decision: Naming sections with "THE ___" convention (THE ESSENCE, THE SCALE, THE LOVE) creates a narrative arc that transforms a scrollable landing page into a story. Visitors don't just read facts — they experience a curated journey.

Improvement Roadmap Prioritized design and UX recommendations across 3 effort tiers

Recommendations are graded by impact, effort, and alignment with the site's positioning goals.

🔴 High Priority — Quick Wins

1. Add a visible Event Date + Registration CTA above the fold. The hero section lacks a specific date for WOW 2026. Students visiting can't immediately answer "when is it?" — the most basic conversion question. Add a sticky date badge and primary "Get Tickets / Register" button in the hero.
2. Implement Scroll Spy on Navigation. The active "HOME" pill doesn't update as users scroll. Adding a scrollspy effect (Intersection Observer) would make navigation contextually helpful — users always know which section they're in.
3. Mobile-first audit of the 4-column stat grid. On mobile, 4 colored stat cards in a row will collapse poorly. Each card needs defined responsive breakpoints: 4-col desktop → 2-col tablet → 1-col mobile with stacked layout.
4. Accessibility pass: Color contrast on yellow buttons. #fbbc04 on white backgrounds and black text have a contrast ratio of ~6.5:1 (fine), but the small uppercase label text on yellow stat badges may fall below WCAG AA. Run a Lighthouse accessibility audit.

🟡 Medium Priority — Design Depth

5. Establish a formal Design Token system. Colors, spacing, and typography are visually consistent but likely not tokenized in the codebase. Define CSS custom properties for all brand colors, spacing values, and border radii to enable safe, scalable updates across years.
6. Introduce a Speaker Profile Page. Speakers are shown as thumbnail cards but there's no click-through to bio/talk details. A dedicated speaker page (or modal) with photo, bio, talk title, and social links would improve conversion for prospective attendees choosing sessions.
7. Dark mode support. The footer and FAQ Terminal already use dark backgrounds — and the audience (developers) skews heavily toward dark mode. Implementing a proper dark mode toggle with prefers-color-scheme detection would feel natural for this audience.
8. Session / Agenda page. The site promotes the event but provides no schedule information. Even a rough "Day 1 / Day 2" agenda section would significantly increase registration intent — attendees want to know what they're signing up for.

🟢 Low Priority — Enhancement Layer

9. Animated entrance for Impact Stats section. The 10,000+, 25+, 35+, 2 numbers are static. A count-up animation triggered on scroll-into-view (using IntersectionObserver + requestAnimationFrame) would make the scale feel earned and dramatic — a standard pattern for event sites.
10. Photo gallery hover states. The gallery star/bookmark icons suggest an interactive layer but no hover state is visible. Adding a subtle overlay + icon reveal on hover (or a lightbox on click) would make the gallery section feel complete rather than decorative.
11. Strengthen the FAQ Terminal interaction. The terminal is excellent conceptually. Enhance it with: typing animation for the question, a loading spinner between Q and A, and keyboard shortcut hints (e.g., ↑↓ to navigate). This makes it a genuinely delightful micro-interaction.
12. Consider a "Volunteer / Core Team" section. The site celebrates speakers and sponsors but the organizing team is invisible. A "Meet the Builders" section with headshots and roles would humanize the event, increase community trust, and help in recruiting future organizers.

WOW Pune's site is visually sophisticated for a student-run event — the Google color system, neo-brutalist card language, and terminal FAQ are all strong differentiated choices. The primary opportunity lies in completing the conversion funnel: adding clear event dates, a registration path, and an agenda would transform the site from an impressive showcase into a genuine attendee acquisition engine.
